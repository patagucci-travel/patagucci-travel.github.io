Coronavirus and the Airlines

George He • April 10, 2020• Blogs

Flights are cancelled, hotels are closed, and so are your dreams for a vacation this summer. Travelers should be wary that travel restrictions and flight cancellations are likely here to stay for a while, as the U.S. Federal government prepares itself for up to 18 months of social restrictions before a coronavirus vaccine is developed and tested. While the focus of many travelers has been to cancel existing plans, we are beginning to settle into the new norm. One of the most common questions that frequent travelers have begun to wonder about is what will become of their hard-earned miles, points and elite status benefits.

Many airlines have been announcing updated policies regarding their loyalty programs in order to maintain their customer base and ensure that those customers return to start spending on flights and nights once they are able to travel again.

Here are the most up-to-date details for extending points expiration, elite status, and other benefits like companion tickets and suite upgrades:

Delta

On April 5, 2020, Delta announced that SkyMiles members would retain their current Medallion elite status through January 2022, which equates to a full additional year. In addition to that, the airline said that any Medallion Qualification Miles members earn in 2020 and 2021 (including those that folks with certain Delta credit cards can accrue through spending) will be combined toward their Medallion qualification for 2022.

Fliers will still have to meet the Medallion Qualification Dollar requirements in 2021 for the 2022 elite year, though, which means still spending between $3,000-$15,000 on airfare next year. Delta also plans to lengthen the validity of certain perks like airport lounge memberships, upgrade certificates, travel vouchers and companion certificates for at least six months, depending on when they were originally due to end.

A complete list of announced updates can be found below:

  • All Medallion Status for 2020 will be automatically extended for the 2021 Medallion Year.
  • All Medallion Qualification Miles (MQMs) from 2020 are being rolled over to 2021 to qualify for 2022 Medallion Status.
  • Delta Sky Club Individual and Executive memberships with an expiration of March 1, 2020, or later will receive six additional months of Delta Sky Club access beyond their expiration date.
  • Delta SkyMiles American Express Card Members:
  • If you have one of the following in your SkyMiles profile “My Wallet” that is valid now or has expired since March 1, 2020, we are extending the expiration dates to give you additional time to enjoy your benefits:
  • Delta SkyMiles Gold Card Members with a $100 Delta flight credit will get a six-month extension beyond their current expiration date.
  • Delta SkyMiles Platinum Card Members with Companion Certificates with an original expiration date between March 1 and June 30, 2020, can use them when they book and fly by Dec. 31, 2020, and those that expire between July 1, 2020, and April 1, 2021, will receive an additional six months beyond the current expiration date.
  • Delta SkyMiles Reserve Card Members with Companion Certificates with an original expiration date between March 1 and June 30, 2020, can use them when they book and fly by Dec. 31, 2020, and those that expire between July 1, 2020, and April 1, 2021, will receive an additional six months beyond the current expiration date.
  • Delta SkyMiles Reserve Card Members will also get a six-month extension to use their Delta Sky Club One-Time Guest Passes beyond their current expiration date.
  • Upgrade Certificates or $200 Travel Vouchers with an original expiration date between March 1 and June 30, 2020, are extended – now, they can be booked and flown by Dec. 31, 2020. And, SkyMiles Members with Upgrade Certificates or $200 Travel Vouchers that expire after June 30, 2020, will receive an additional six months beyond the current expiration date.
  • SkyMiles Select members will receive a six-month extension to the Priority Boarding benefit and any unused drink vouchers.
United

United announced on April 5, 2020 that the airline will extend MileagePlus members’ current level of Premier elite status by an additional year, through the end of January 2022. This currently applies to levels from Premier Silver up to Premier 1K, but not Global Services. United also halving flight and spending qualification requirements for members who aim to earn Premier status in 2020 for 2021. United also plans to extend all its annual subscriptions by six months. Those include United Club membership, Economy Plus, Wi-Fi plans and checked-bag subscriptions. A list of changes can be found below:

  • If you have current 2020 Premier status, it will be extended to January 31, 2022. At a minimum, you will enjoy the same published status next year that you have today, up to and including Premier 1K®.
  • For the 2021 status year, United is reducing thresholds for Premier qualification by 50% for each status level, to make reaching an even higher tier easier. You'll still need a minimum of four flight segments on United or United Express®.
  • Doubling PQP for United Explorer cards and quadrupling PQP for the United Club cards.
  • 1K® and Platinum members, we're increasing your ability to upgrade by extending PlusPoints expiration dates by six months and expanding Skip Waitlist.
  • Extending all annual membership and subscription benefits by six months.
  • All electronic travel certificates now have 24 months to be used
  • Waiving all award redeposit fees for travel through end of May 2020.
  • Waiving all redeposit fees for award ticket cancellations made more than 30 days before departure for the remainder of 2020.
  • Making it easier to earn status in 2021 for the 2022 program year [Changes to come].
Alaska Airlines

Alaska Airlines announced on April 7, 2020 that Alaska Mileage Plan status will be extended another year, through December 31, 2021. For those looking to earn status in 2020 for 2021, the airline has not yet made plans to adjust its mileage or flight requirements, though it is possible that Alaska will award even more elite-qualifying miles or segments than usual for flights on its own metal as well as on certain partners. Alaska points will still expire  after 24 months of account inactivity. A full list can be found below:

  • Current Mileage Plan status will be extended through December 31, 2021
  • Members who enrolled in a status match challenge between December 1, 2019 and March 31, 2020 will have their trial status extended through the end of 2021.
  • Companion certificates set to expire in 2020 have their expiration dates extended, but owners will need to book by December 31, 2020 for travel through November 26, 2021.
Virgin Atlantic

Virgin Atlantic announced on April 6, 2020 that it was extending status for Gold and Silver members by six months. Members will get an additional six months added to their membership year to meet those requirements.

In addition to extending the status and requalification period for elites, Flying Club is also extending the validity of some elite benefits.

While most (if not all) airlines have started to offer waived fees for cancellations and modifications, not all airlines have announced changes to their loyalty programs. Among the notable airlines missing from this list are American Airlines, Southwest, and JetBlue.
