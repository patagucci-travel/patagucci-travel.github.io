New Zealand

Jeffrey Lin• November 10, 2019• Blogs
Hiking, food, bungy jumps, and much more to see in New Zealand. October 2019. Temperature in the 50s to 70s.
Review: Mt. Cook Hike - 8.5/10

The Mt hook hike passes through 3 bridges with very minimal incline and feeds to a lake, the views are beautiful throughout, but don’t vary too much so the less movement inclined can definitely turn around after the first bridge.

On the drive to Queenstown from Mt. Cook, you pass by lake Pukaki, where there’s a visitor center that sells Mt. Cook Alpine Salmon. This is some of the freshest salmon I’ve ever tasted, my only regret here was not buying more, although there was no shortage of good food in Queenstown (more about that later)

Not to mention the amazing views: Pretty good place for a pit stop

Review: AJ Hackett Bungy Kawarau Bridge - 10/10

The original bungy jump, a must do at an iconic location.

They advertise a water option, but it is actually dependent on the season and the water levels. If you’re on the lighter side it may not be possible. They said I had a chance with enough momentum (finally the hard work eating all that delicious food paid off!)

Pro Tip: Don’t look down when you’re about to jump :)

I was going to jump up to build momentum, but its drop starts to look quite far away if you stare down too much, I think I could’ve done it if I stayed focused on staring ahead. The beginning freefall is an incredibly thrilling feeling as you can see the ground fast approaching.

You can jump again for a cheap 50 nz, which I definitely wanted to do, but I don’t think my parents could handle watching me jump off a ledge again.

Review: Fergburger - 10/10

After the thrill of a bungy jump, you need some good old thick calories to feed that adrenaline rush. No place better than Fergburger, where the line is wrapped around the corner no matter what time it is. Their bread is fresh and their meat is amazing.

Our theory on their secret was just take a look at the scenery and the numerous lambs, cows, and animals grazing on the grass on the drive through: happy meat tastes good!

But with their quality ingredients also comes amazing artisanal cooking skills, resulting in a really delectable meal.

And here’s the real stamp of approval: my parents, who normally hate burgers, ate here for all their meals for 3 days.
